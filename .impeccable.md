## Design Context

### Users
Residents and guests of StarCity Estate in Yangon, Myanmar — 9,000+ people across three developments (City Loft, Estella, ARA). They use the app on mobile to manage their apartment lifecycle: paying bills, submitting maintenance tickets, booking facilities, and staying connected with community announcements. Primary context: at home, on their phone, often in the evening.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, trustworthy, residential** — feels like a well-run community. Approachable, reliable, home-like. Not a corporate tool, but a neighborhood companion.

### Emotional Goal
**Community pride** — residents should feel they're part of something well-managed and special. The app should reinforce that StarCity is a premium place to live.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Warm residential with premium touches — not luxury-cold, not generic-SaaS
- **Theme**: Light mode primary (residents browsing at home), dark mode supported
- **Colors**: Deep teal primary + warm gold accents (established brand palette)
- **Font**: Plus Jakarta Sans (current) — warm, modern, readable
- **References**: Well-designed community/property apps (not corporate dashboards)
- **Anti-references**: Generic corporate SaaS, cheap/cluttered layouts, over-designed/flashy interfaces

### Design Principles
1. **Residential warmth over corporate polish** — every screen should feel like home, not an office portal
2. **Clear over clever** — information hierarchy serves the task, not the designer's ego
3. **Mobile-first always** — 90%+ usage is on phones; desktop is a nice-to-have
4. **Quiet confidence** — premium quality shown through spacing, typography, and restraint, not flashy effects
5. **Community-centric** — the app represents StarCity's identity; design should make residents proud
